2022 - 2022
Made it to FANG as a Data Engineer! I built some really cool data pipelines and even got the return offer. However it didn't feel right. I had a deep passion for machine learning, and if I stayed I wouldn't get to do much more than SQL queries. Extremely hard decision.
2020 - 2023
I got the opportunity to go back to Brigham Young University to work with Dr. Gus Williams again. This time on a NASA sponsored project to do time series imputation for groundwater wells. The project was focused on analyzing the change of groundwater in Western Africa, but I thought, If I could do it for a specific country, why not generalize it and apply it to anywhere in the world.

This was the best experience I could have asked for. I got to mentor students in Data Science while learning more about machine learning myself. I because with linear regression which became neural networks, images began to be analyzed using CNNs, and at a certain point Time series analysis became sequence modeling which became NLP.

I accidentally fell in love with the application of machine learning to real world problems, little did I know that I had set myself up for a career as a Machine Learning Engineer
2019 - 2020
Rubber hit the road, litterally. I designed drainage systems, stormwater management systems, and other civil engineering systems for a variety of projects. Mostly behemoth sized highway and lightrail projects.

I found out that civil engineering and construction, in practice, is not the most innovative field. Most of the cutting edge modeling and simulation I learned in school was not being used. I was a bit disappointed, but I learned so much about how the world around me was built.

Along the way my boss figured out I could code, and I was assigned to work on research projects focusing on Computer Vision, Natural Language Processing, and Optimization to expediate design and increase safety for workers. After a while I decided that programming was more fun than civil engineering, and there were plenty of people who could do the civil engineering part just as well, or better, than I could. So I decided to shift my focus on programming and data science.
